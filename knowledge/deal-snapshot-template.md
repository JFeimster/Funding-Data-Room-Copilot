# Funding Deal Snapshot Template

## Purpose and Limitations

The deal snapshot is a one-page operating summary. It helps a business owner, broker, processor, or reviewer understand the file without rebuilding the story from scattered messages and documents.

It is not an underwriting memo, approval recommendation, or legal conclusion.

## Business-Owner Snapshot

Focus on:

- business identity
- funding purpose
- requested amount
- documents collected
- missing items
- cash-flow context
- next-best action
- privacy and human-review status

## Broker / Processor Snapshot

Add:

- client ID
- deal owner
- referral source
- workflow stage
- document completeness
- Blockers
- questions pending
- follow-up owner
- submission destination
- handoff date
- human reviewer

- recurring revenue percentage
- accounts receivable
- current contracts or backlog

- seasonality
- preferred funding structure
- referral source
- renewal date
- last contact date

Optional fields should be included only when relevant and supported.

## Positive Factors

Positive factors should describe confirmed operating facts, not approval signals.

Examples:

- requested document period is complete
- business identity is consistent
- use of funds is specific
- current financial reports are available
- receivables are documented
- cash-flow event has supporting context
- debt schedule is current

Avoid phrases such as “strong approval chance” or “guaranteed fit.”

## Potential Concerns

State concerns neutrally:

- incomplete statement period
- unexplained revenue decline
- conflicting ownership records
- outdated P&L
- unclear debt payment frequency
- sensitive data exposed
- requested amount differs across sources

Do not diagnose fraud or make underwriting conclusions.

## Provisional Assumptions

Use this section only for noncritical missing facts.

### Provisional — Assumptions Used

| Assumption | Why Needed | Confirm | Affected Output |
|---|---|---|---|
|  |  |  |  |

Identity, ownership, privacy, and authenticity conflicts require a focused question or human escalation rather than an assumption.

## Next-Best Action

Recommend one primary next action, then up to three secondary actions.

Example:

1. Obtain the complete May statement.
2. Update the YTD P&L.
3. Add a factual explanation for the equipment purchase.

## Human Review Gate

Use one classification:

- **Ready for Human Review**
- **Hold — Blocker Present**
- **Provisional — Confirmation Needed**

Never use “approved,” “pre-approved,” or “declined” as the Copilot’s conclusion.

## Completed Fictional Example

**Fictional example for workflow training only. It does not imply a funding outcome.**

### Funding Deal Snapshot — Ridgeway Electric LLC

**Business Name:** Ridgeway Electric LLC  
**Legal Entity:** Virginia limited liability company  
**DBA:** None reported  
**Industry:** Electrical contractor  
**Location:** Richmond, Virginia  
**Time in Business:** Four years, reported by owner  
**Owner Name:** Morgan Reed, pending ownership-document confirmation  

**Requested Amount:** $80,000  
**Minimum Useful Amount:** $55,000  
**Use of Funds:** Equipment purchase, supplier deposits, and payroll support during two scheduled commercial projects  

**Monthly Revenue Context:** Revenue varies by project completion and customer payment timing. The owner reports a recent increase in contracted work.  
**Bank Statement Period:** February through June received  
**Bank Statement Notes:** The May statement is missing two pages. A large May withdrawal is reported as an equipment deposit and requires a supporting invoice.  
**Existing Debt:** Equipment loan listed; payment frequency still needs confirmation  

**Documents Received:**

- formation document
- February–June bank statements
- current equipment quote
- YTD P&L
- customer contracts

**Documents Missing:**

- complete May bank statement
- current debt schedule
- confirmed ownership schedule

**Positive Factors:**

- specific use of funds
- current equipment quote
- customer contracts available
- YTD P&L received

**Potential Concerns:**

- incomplete May statement
- ownership confirmation pending
- debt payment frequency missing

### Readiness Gaps

| Gap | Priority | Why It Matters | Next Action | Owner | Human Review |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| May statement missing pages | Blocker | Review period is incomplete | Download full provider PDF | Business owner | Yes |
| Ownership schedule unconfirmed | Blocker | Authority and ownership must be verified | Confirm current ownership document | Owner and processor | Yes |
| Debt frequency missing | Fix Before Submission | Payment load is unclear | Add payment frequency to debt schedule | Bookkeeper | Yes |
| Equipment-deposit note unsupported | Fix Before Submission | Large withdrawal needs factual support | Attach vendor invoice | Business owner | Yes |

**Recommended Next Step:** Resolve the two Blockers before treating the file as ready for human submission review.

**Human Review Required:** Yes  
**QA Classification:** Hold — Blocker Present
